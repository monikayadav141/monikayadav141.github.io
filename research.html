Ethnography of Migrant Construction Workers

Methods

Ethnographic fieldwork, semi-structured interviews, participant observation, multilingual fieldwork, qualitative coding.

What I Delivered

  • Mapped workflows and peer-led training on a large infrastructure site over 20 weeks of observation and 40+ interviews.
  • Identified risk regulation patterns and informal mobility ladders shaping who learns what—and when.
  • Converted unstructured notes into a systems view of skill formation that informed training sequence redesigns.

Impact

  • Surfaced friction points in onboarding and coordination, informing safer, more efficient task handoffs.
  • Built a hard-to-access dataset and manuscript on informal learning and workforce mobility (under review).

Teaching Assessment Fellowship

Methods

Program evaluation, mixed-methods assessment (quant + qual), semi-structured interviews, survey analysis, feasibility study, AI tool evaluation.

What I Delivered

  • Built assessment plans for faculty-led interventions; analyzed exam performance and survey data.
  • Ran a feasibility study on AI-generated learning plans, documenting hallucination risks and prompt brittleness.
  • Partnered with CTL fellows to evaluate Graduate Student Teaching Orientation and segment engagement gaps.

Impact

  • Guided leadership decisions on AI adoption timelines and safeguards.
  • Produced briefs that shaped program redesign for stronger reach across STEM and humanities instructors.

Meaning-Making & Communication Studies

Focus

Interviews and observation across multiple projects to understand how people construct meaning, interpret messages, and build trust in everyday contexts.

Sample Insights

  • Uncovered emotional and contextual cues that shape message comprehension, revealing failure points in clarity.
  • Developed user segments showing how different groups process or resist information, guiding tailored engagement.
  • Identified trust-building moments that improved framing and communication strategies.
