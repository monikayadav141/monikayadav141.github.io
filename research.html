Ethnography of Migrant Construction Workers

Methods

Ethnographic fieldwork, semi-structured interviews, participant observation, multilingual fieldwork, qualitative coding.

What I Delivered

  • Mapped workflows and peer-led training on a large infrastructure site over 20 weeks of observation and 40+ interviews.
  • Identified risk regulation patterns and informal mobility ladders shaping who learns what—and when.
  • Converted unstructured notes into a systems view of skill formation that informed training sequence redesigns.

Impact

  • Surfaced friction points in onboarding and coordination, informing safer, more efficient task handoffs.
  • Built a hard-to-access dataset and manuscript on informal learning and workforce mobility (under review).

Labor & Litigation Project (MA Thesis)

Methods

Original dataset construction, scraping and integrating legal records, longitudinal analysis (1989–2016), statistical modeling.

What I Delivered

  • Scraped federal court records and matched them with NLRB election data to build a 25-year panel across 50 states.
  • Ran regression models to estimate how union density shapes legal mobilization among non-union workers.
  • Cleaned and validated tens of thousands of filings, turning noisy administrative data into analyzable signals.

Impact

  • Demonstrated a statistically significant link between labor ecosystems and individual legal action.
  • Showcased large-scale data wrangling and applied causal reasoning on messy real-world sources.

Large-Scale Misinformation Field Experiment

Methods

Randomized field experiment (N≈1,300), structural modeling, survey design, mobile-based delivery.

What I Delivered

  • Co-designed multi-wave interventions and weekly deployments for mobile fact-checking digests.
  • Led field ops and research interns; maintained engagement and data quality over months of rollouts.
  • Applied structural modeling to separate improved discernment from generalized skepticism.

Impact

  • One of the largest misinformation experiments in India; improved users' ability to evaluate online content.
  • Produced briefs for tech partners translating causal results into product and communication implications.

Survey Experiment on Accountability

Methods

Original survey experiment, multi-round conjoint design, treatment manipulation, Qualtrics programming (JS/CSS), AMCE estimation.

What I Delivered

  • Programmed a dynamic, multi-round conjoint with real-time scorecards and custom logic in Qualtrics.
  • Ran pilots, cleaned multi-round user-level data, and conducted AMCE and interaction modeling.
  • Documented pre-analysis plans and refined interface copy to keep tasks intuitive.

Impact

  • Revealed how accountability changes resource-allocation decisions and which attributes drive choices.
  • Improved task comprehension and completion rates through UX refinements to complex conjoint flows.
